Conclusion

The 1965 war ended without a clear victor, but its consequences were far-reaching. Pakistan’s hopes for a decisive political and territorial gain remained unfulfilled, while India restored its confidence after the 1962 setback with China.

The Tashkent Agreement brought peace but not closure. The sudden death of Indian Prime Minister Lal Bahadur Shastri in Tashkent added mystery and tension to the aftermath.

In Pakistan, the return from Tashkent marked the beginning of deep political rifts. Zulfikar Ali Bhutto emerged as a populist leader, challenging President Ayub Khan’s policies and paving the way for a new political era.

The war may have ceased, but its echoes reshaped the subcontinent’s destiny.
