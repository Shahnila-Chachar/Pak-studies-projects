Tashkent Agreement & Its Aftermath

  • Signed on 10 January 1966 in Tashkent, mediated by USSR.
  • Leaders: Ayub Khan and Lal Bahadur Shastri.
  • Both nations agreed to ceasefire and troop withdrawal.
  • Restoration of diplomatic relations and peaceful dialogue.
  • Viewed in Pakistan as a diplomatic setback.
  • Led to political rift between Ayub Khan and Bhutto.
  • Shastri’s death in Tashkent added to the tragedy.
