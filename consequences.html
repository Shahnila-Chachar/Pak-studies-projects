Consequences of the 1965 War

  • No significant territorial change occurred after the war.
  • Both countries faced heavy human and economic losses.
  • The war exposed military and intelligence weaknesses on both sides.
  • It led to greater military buildup in both nations.
  • Pakistan’s economy suffered due to increased defense spending.
  • India initiated major defense reforms after the conflict.
  • The war concluded with the Tashkent Agreement (1966) under UN mediation.
